What is Coral Bleaching?

Coral bleaching occurs when corals lose their vibrant colors and turn white due to stress, often caused by rising sea temperatures.

About Coral Bleaching

Corals have a symbiotic relationship with algae called zooxanthellae, which give corals their color and energy. When stressed, corals expel these algae, leading to bleaching.

Causes of Coral Bleaching

  • Rising sea temperatures
  • Ocean acidification
  • Pollution and runoff
  • Overfishing and physical damage

Effects of Coral Bleaching

Bleaching weakens coral reefs, reducing biodiversity and affecting marine life, fisheries, and coastal protection.

What You Can Do

  • Reduce your carbon footprint
  • Avoid single-use plastics
  • Choose sustainable seafood
  • Spread awareness
