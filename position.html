Coral bleaching occurs when corals expel the symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) living in their tissues. These algae provide corals with food and their vibrant colors. Without them, corals turn white and become more vulnerable to disease and death.

üíß Main Causes

  • Rising ocean temperatures due to global warming
  • Pollution and poor water quality
  • Increased solar radiation (especially UV)
  • Extreme low tides

Coral reefs support about 25% of all marine life. When corals bleach and die, entire ecosystems are affected ‚Äî including fish, sea turtles, and even humans who rely on reefs for food and tourism.
